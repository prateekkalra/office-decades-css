• 1950s

    Heavy wooden desks, click-clack typewriters, rotary phones, filing cabinets and ash trays define the Mad-Men-era office.

  • 1960s

    Curved modernist furniture, IBM Selectric typewriters, lava lamps and psychedelic posters bring the optimistic Space-Age vibe.

  • 1970s

    Wood-panelled walls, green-glow terminals, potted plants and a disco-ball shine capture the groovy seventies workspace.

  • 1980s

    Neon Memphis patterns, bulky CRT monitors, brick cell phones and power-suit culture dominate the high-octane eighties.

  • 1990s

    Beige tower PCs running Windows 95, ergonomic peripherals, "You've Got Mail" pings and Tamagotchis typify the dot-com boom.

  • 2000s

    Silver flat-screens, flip phones, iPods, Aeron chairs and "Keep Calm" posters signal the sleek new millennium office.

  • 2010s

    MacBooks, standing desks, ping-pong breaks, succulents and Slack notifications epitomise the casual startup era.

  • 2020s

    Dual-monitor video calls, hand-sanitiser stations, ring lights and "Work From Anywhere" signs reflect the hybrid-work decade.
